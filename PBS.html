Education Careers

Principles of teaching

This course provides an introduction to the field of teaching, focusing on the development of effective instructional strategies, classroom management, lesson planning, and educational philosophies. Students gain foundational knowledge and explore the various roles and responsibilities of educators.

Child and Adolescent development

Explores the stages of physical, cognitive, social, and emotional development from birth through adolescence. The course emphasizes theories of development, influential factors such as family and culture, and strategies to support learning and growth in educational settings.

Teaching and learning

Covers essential instructional methods, student engagement strategies, classroom assessments, and differentiated learning approaches. This course prepares aspiring teachers to create inclusive and dynamic learning environments.

Cosmetology

Principles of Barbering and Cosmetology

Introduces the history, ethics, and foundational principles of barbering and cosmetology. Students learn safety and sanitation practices, basic hair care services, and explore trends within the beauty industry to understand client needs and expectations.

Barbering and Cosmetology Fundamentals

Focuses on hands-on training in cutting, styling, coloring, and skincare techniques. Emphasis is placed on proper tool usage, client consultations, and service delivery in a professional salon setting, building foundational cosmetology skills.

Advanced Cosmetology

This advanced course builds upon previous cosmetology skills, introducing techniques such as chemical treatments, advanced hairstyling, nail art, and salon management practices. Students refine their artistry and gain insight into business operations in the industry.

Law Enforcement

Principles of Criminal Education

Introduces students to the criminal justice system, examining the roles of law enforcement, courts, and corrections. The course covers legal ethics, the history of criminal justice, and the impact of crime on society.

Law Enforcement Fundamentals

This course offers an in-depth study of law enforcement practices, criminal investigations, public safety procedures, and ethical conduct within the field. Students learn about crime prevention, evidence collection, and officer responsibilities.

Corrections and Correctional Awareness

Focuses on correctional institutions, including prison management, rehabilitation methods, inmate rights, and societal reintegration programs. It provides an understanding of the challenges and duties faced by corrections officers.

Pre-law

Law Education

Offers an overview of the U.S. legal system, including the structure of courts, constitutional law, criminal and civil cases, and the role of citizens in a democratic society. Students engage in case studies and legal simulations to enhance their understanding.

AP Languange and Composition

Develops advanced skills in critical reading, rhetorical analysis, and persuasive writing. The course prepares students to analyze complex texts and construct coherent arguments, crucial skills for those considering legal or advocacy roles.

ACP Govt/ We the People

Focuses on constitutional principles, government structure, and civic engagement. This course often involves in-depth projects, debates, or mock trials that explore constitutional issues and civic responsibility.

Psychology

Sociology or Psychology

Students may choose to study either Sociology, which focuses on social behavior, cultural norms, and societal institutions, or Psychology, which delves into human behavior, cognition, and emotions. Each course emphasizes analytical and observational skills.

AP Statistics

Provides a deep dive into statistical analysis and data interpretation, including probability, data collection, and hypothesis testing. This course is particularly relevant to psychological research and real-world applications in data-driven fields.

AP/ACP/IB Psychology or DC Anatomy

Depending on selection, students may engage in a rigorous study of psychological concepts and theories, including cognition, personality, and human development, or they may focus on anatomy, learning about human body systems in depth, often with a clinical or biological perspective.

Goverment & Public Administration

ACP Buisness

Introduces students to essential business concepts such as economics, marketing, management, and entrepreneurship. The course aims to equip students with knowledge about the business environment and organizational strategies.

AP Macro/AP Micro or AP Statistics

Covers economic theories, policies, and their applications to real-world scenarios (Macro/Microeconomics) or focuses on data analysis and statistical applications (Statistics). Each course provides analytical tools beneficial for government or policy careers.

ACP Speech/AP Govt

Develops skills in public speaking, debate, and governmental analysis. Students study political systems, public policy, and constitutional law, enhancing their ability to articulate viewpoints and influence public opinion.

Culinary

Principles of Culinary and Hospitality

Introduces students to the culinary and hospitality industry, focusing on kitchen safety, sanitation, food preparation, and customer service. The course provides a foundation for pursuing careers in food service and hospitality management.

Nutrition

Covers the science of nutrition, exploring dietary needs, meal planning, and food science. Students learn how nutrition impacts health, performance, and well-being, along with principles for designing balanced meals.

Culinary Art

Focuses on advanced food preparation techniques, including baking, cooking, and international cuisine. Students learn about menu planning, cost control, and kitchen management, preparing them for roles in professional culinary settings.
