Auto & Collision Repair

Principles of Collision Repair

Provides students an overview of the operating, electrical, and general maintenance systems of the modern automobile. Students will be introduced to the safety and operation of equipment and tools used in the automotive collision industry.

Repair & Paint Fundamentals

Introduces the types of fiberglass and plastic materials used in auto body repair and considerations for automotive painting. Students will explore methods for repairing fiberglass and plastic damage, like welding, reinforcing, repairing holes, and retexturing plastic.

Automotive Body Repair

Provides students with an understanding of the materials, measuring, welding, and information resources applicable to collision repair. Students will study steel and aluminum dent repair, including the welding practices commonly performed within an automotive repair environment.

Automotive

Principles of Automotive Services

Introduction to automotive systems, maintenance procedures, and industry safety practices. Students learn fundamental concepts and hands-on skills essential for automotive service.

Brake Systems

Comprehensive study of automotive brake systems, including hydraulic theory, diagnosis, and repair of disc and drum brakes. Covers anti-lock systems and modern electronic brake controls.

Steering and Suspensions

Detailed exploration of vehicle steering and suspension systems, including alignment principles, electronic steering, and modern suspension designs. Focuses on diagnostic and repair procedures.

Welding

Principles of Welding

Introduction to welding processes, safety procedures, and basic metallurgy. Students learn fundamental techniques and industry standards for various welding applications.

Shielded Metal Arc Welding

Advanced study of SMAW techniques, including vertical and overhead positions. Covers electrode selection, joint preparation, and quality control methods.

Gas Welding

Comprehensive training in gas welding and cutting processes. Includes torch manipulation, joint design, and proper gas mixture selection for various materials.

Construction Trades

Principles of Construction Trade

Foundation course covering basic construction concepts, safety practices, and tool usage. Introduces students to various construction career paths and industry standards.

General Carpentry

Comprehensive study of carpentry basics, including wood properties, measurement, cutting techniques, and joint construction. Covers both hand and power tool operations.

Carpentry - Framing and Finishing

Advanced carpentry techniques focusing on structural framing and interior finishing. Includes roof systems, stair construction, and cabinet installation.
