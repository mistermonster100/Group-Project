Emergency Medical Technician

The Emergency Medical Technician (EMT) course provides students with the essential knowledge and skills required to respond to medical emergencies and provide pre-hospital care. Topics covered include patient assessment, airway management, CPR and advanced cardiac life support, trauma care, emergency pharmacology, and medical emergencies such as respiratory, cardiovascular, and neurological conditions. Pre-Medical

Adv. Anatomy/Physiology

The Advanced Anatomy and Physiology course is designed to provide students with a deeper understanding of the structure and function of the human body, building upon foundational knowledge in basic anatomy and physiology. This course delves into the complex interrelationships between the body's organ systems, emphasizing how they work together to maintain homeostasis and support life.

AP/ACP Biology

The AP/ACP Biology course offers an in-depth exploration of biological principles and concepts, preparing students for both advanced college-level biology studies and the AP Biology exam. The course covers a wide range of topics including cell biology, genetics, evolution, ecology, and the molecular mechanisms that govern life. Students will study biological systems from the molecular level up to the ecosystem level, understanding the complexity and interconnectedness of life processes.

AP/ACP Chemistry

The AP/ACP Chemistry course provides an intensive study of the principles of chemistry at a college level, designed for students preparing for the AP Chemistry exam and those pursuing advanced studies in science. Topics covered include atomic structure, chemical bonding, thermodynamics, kinetics, equilibrium, acid-base chemistry, and redox reactions.

Biomedical Science

Principles of Biomedical

Principles of Biomedical Science introduces students to the interdisciplinary field of biomedical science, where they explore key concepts in biology, chemistry, and health science to understand the human body and its systems. The course covers foundational topics such as human anatomy and physiology, cellular biology, microbiology, genetics, and the biomedical research process.

Human Body Systems

Human Body Systems provides students with an in-depth study of the human body’s structure and function, emphasizing the interconnectivity of its various systems. Topics include the skeletal, muscular, cardiovascular, respiratory, digestive, nervous, and endocrine systems, among others. Through hands-on activities and laboratory experiments, students explore how these systems work independently and together to maintain homeostasis and overall health.

Medical Interventions

Medical Intervention is an advanced course that explores the role of medical professionals in diagnosing, treating, and managing diseases and conditions. Students will investigate a variety of medical interventions, including diagnostic procedures, surgical techniques, pharmaceutical treatments, and innovative medical technologies.

Exercise Science

Principles of Exercise Science

Principles of Exercise Science introduces students to the foundational concepts of exercise physiology, biomechanics, and kinesiology. This course covers the scientific principles behind human movement, physical fitness, and the impact of exercise on overall health and performance.

Kinesiology

Kinesiology is the study of human movement, focusing on the mechanics of body motion and the physiological effects of exercise. This course provides a comprehensive understanding of the anatomical and biomechanical principles that govern human movement, including the structure and function of muscles, bones, joints, and the nervous system.

Human Performance

Human Performance focuses on the physiological, biomechanical, and psychological factors that contribute to an individual’s ability to perform physical tasks, particularly in athletic and fitness contexts. This course explores the science behind optimizing performance through training, nutrition, and recovery. Topics include muscular strength, endurance, cardiovascular fitness, flexibility, and motor skills, as well as how the body adapts to various types of physical activity.
